The Core System: Clothing & Gear

Your strategy should be built around a small collection of versatile, high-quality items. Think in terms of a capsule wardrobe where everything works together.

The 5-4-3-2-1 Clothing Formula

This is a starting point for a temperate climate, adjust as needed. The trick is to do laundry every few days.

  • 5x Underwear & Socks
  • 4x Tops (mix of short and long sleeve)
  • 3x Bottoms (e.g., travel pants, shorts, dressier slacks)
  • 2x Pairs of Shoes (one for walking, one other)
  • 1x Outer Layer (e.g., packable jacket)

The Layering System is Key

Instead of bulky items for different weather, pack layers you can combine:

  1. Base Layer: A moisture-wicking shirt (e.g., merino wool).
  2. Mid-Layer: An insulating layer like a fleece or lightweight down vest.
  3. Outer Shell: A waterproof and windproof jacket.

This three-part system can handle a surprisingly wide range of temperatures and conditions.

Packing Techniques

Rolling vs. Folding

Rolling clothes tightly is generally superior. It minimizes wrinkles and saves a significant amount of space.

Packing Cubes

These are non-negotiable. They compress clothing and keep your bag organized, acting like portable drawers.
